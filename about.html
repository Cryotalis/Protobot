About Protobot

I became interested in learning how to create my own Discord bot near the end of 2018. At the time, all I wanted to do was create a “mock” command, which would take some text and randomly capitalize the letters, emulating the mocking spongebob meme. After my first attempt failed, I gave up, but returned to the project 3 months later, this time succeeding. After this, I sought to replace the dd2tools bot, which had been retired just a month prior due to a lack of funding and interest from its creator. To do this, I created the first version of Protobot, written with NodeJS & Discord.js in Javascript.


The primary feature of the dd2tools bot was its ability to pull up Shard information. To recreate this, I connected Protobot to a google spreadsheet I already compiled which contained a list of all Shards and Mods, and took some time to set up fuzzy matching to make sure that even when people misspelled a Shard, Protobot would still fetch the correct one. I started development of Protobot on Juicebags’s server, and within a week, developed several other commands in addition to shard and mod - minasc, drakenfrost, mastery, link, news, and a help command. Just a few days later, it would be added to the official Dungeon Defenders server.


Protobot would go on to gain several new commands and lose old ones. After leaving the project for a couple of months, the bot fell into disrepair (in 2021) as it was not updated to the latest version of Discord.js. It would go down for about 2 months, after which I rewrote Protobot in Python with Discord.py instead to learn the language. Just a few months after this, the creator of Discord.py would announce that the library was being put on maintenance mode, at which point I rewrote Protobot again, returning to my roots and this time writing it in TypeScript with Discord.js. This is the version of Protobot that is in use today.
